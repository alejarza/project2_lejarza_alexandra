Education

As an educator in higher ed, I tell my students that you will know what your major is when you are excited to read the textbook and happy to go to class. That's how I learned Psychology was for me. I received my Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology at the University of Miami with 3 minors in French, English, and Family & Human Services. This track naturally led me to develop an interest in counseling. Making individuals more aware of the influence of mental health on our well-being became a priority. I received a Master of Science degree in Counselor Education at Florida International University (FIU).

Working in a college, it made sense to enroll in a doctoral program in Higher Education at FIU to help fulfill what I felt was missing in my career. Based on my work as an educator, my qualitative study focused on the lived experiences of female academic advisors. Through semi-structured interviews, the women discussed their career aspirations, interpersonal relationships between coworkers, and their day-to-day duties. In part, the research intended to highlight an important group of people who are sometimes overlooked in higher education research: staff members.
